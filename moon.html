The Moon

There are many moons in our solar system, which orbit planets, just like planets orbit the Sun. Earth has one Moon, Mars has two small moons, Mercury and Venus don't have any, while Jupiter has at least 63! The Earth's Moon is made up of rocks, bot solid and molten.

Questions

How does the Moon cause tides in our oceans and seas?

Tides are caused by the gravitational force exerted by the Moon on our planet. This force causes the ocean to bulge out in the direction of the Moon, making the tide rise. As the Earth is also pulled towards the Moon, the ocean on the other side also bulges out. So it is high tide there as well. In the region between the to bulges, the water level decreases, causing low tides. Tides are higher in the tropics due to the bulge of the equator. Many forms of life on the coast are tailored to the cycle of tides.

Why does the Moon appear crescent-shaped at times?

The shape of the Moon as seen from the Earth keeps changing. The changing shapes of the Moon are called phases. When the side of the Moon that faces the Earth is turned away from the Sun, we are unable to see the Moon. This phases is called the New Moon. As the Moon travels in its orbit around the Earth, we start to see a small portion of the Moon that is lit up by the Sun. This is called the Cresent Moon. The lit up portion seen by us slowly increases, and we see Half Moon, then a Three-qurater Moon. When Moon completes a half orbit around the Earth, we can see the entire disc lit up by the Sun - the Full Moon.

What are spring tides and neap tides?

When the Sun, the Moon and the Earth are in a straight line, the gravitational force of the Sun strengthens that of the Moon causing tides that higher than usual, these are called spring tides (spring tides have nothing to do with the season of Spring). When the Sun and the Moon are at right angles to the Earth, weaker tides called neap tides, are caused. Tides are important as they wash away the dedris of the coasts.
