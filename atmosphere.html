Earth's Atmosphere

The protective blanket of air that covers the Earth is called the atmosphere. The Earth's atmosphere not only prevents too much heat from entering the planet, but also protects us from asteroids and meteors. The Earth's gravity helps hold the atmosphere in place.

Questions

What are the gases that make up the Earth's atmosphere?

The Earth's atmosphere is composed of many gases. Nitrogen is the main gasstrong> found in the atmosphere. It accounts for about 78% and oxygen makes up 21%. The remaining 1% is a combination of carbon dioxide and water vapour. There are also very small amounts of trace gases like neon and helium that go to make up the Earth's atmosphere.

Why is the ozone layer important?

The ozone layer is important because it stops harmful ultravoilet rays from the Sun from reaching the Earth. If the rays are allowed through the atmosphere, they can cause severe health problems like skin cancer. Chemicals called CFCs have made a hole in the ozone layer above the North and South Poles.

How does stratosphere help us?

The stratosphere is the layer just above the troposphere. It extends upwards from the troposphere to about 50 km above the Earth's surface. Compared to the troposphere whixh is full of moisture, the stratosphere is dry. The stratosphere contians the ozone layer. Ozone absorbs harmful ultraviolet rays from the Sun.


What is the significance of troposphere in the weather pattern?

The troposphere is the layer closest to the Earth's surface, and it is here that weather is created. That means that all weather patterns are made in this layer. Air in the troposphere rises and falls, helping to form clouds, rain and snow. This layer stretches about 8-14.5 km above the sea level.

Is the temperature the same in the different layers in the atmosphere?

The temperature in the troposphere is between -52 and 17 ℃. The temperature in the stratosphere is about -3 ℃. The next layer up, called the mesosphere, is very cold. The temperature here is as low as -93 ℃. In the outer layer of the atmosphere the temperature starts to rise again, because there is more heat from the Sun. Temperatures in the outer layer can be as high as 1,727 ℃.
