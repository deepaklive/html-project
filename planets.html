The Planets

Planets are large masses of matter that orbit around a star. Our solar system consists of 8 planets - Mercury, Venus, Earth, Mars, which are the inner or rocky planets Jupiter, Saturn, Uranus and Neptune, which are the outer planets or gas giants.

Questions

What are the features of a rocky planet?

The rocky planets are made up of rocks and metals like iron and nickel. They are smaller than gas giants but are very heavy. It is because of their weight that rocky planets rotate much slower than the gas giant.

What makes gas giants unique?

The gas giants are bigger in size but lighter, as they are mainly made up of gases and ice particles. In fact, Saturn is so light that it would float if placed in water! Gas giants also spin extremly fast and they have rings around them. Thesse planets do not have a hard surface. Jupiter and Saturn have a semi-liquid centre that is covered by a layer of liquid gas.

What are the different feature of each of the rocky planets?

Mercury is the closest to the Sun and therefore its temperatrue can be high as 467 ℃. Venus is covered with CO₂ containing droplets of sulfuric acid. This traps the Sun's heat and makes Venus hotter than even Mercury. Mars, the red planet, is considered to be the only planet after Earth where life could exist. As far as we know, the Earth is the only planet that supports life.


What are special characteristics of the gas giants?

Jupiter is the largest planet. It rotates faster than any other planet and has the most moons. Saturn is set apart by its beautiful rings, made up of dust particles and pieces of ice. Uranus is a strange planet where seasons last more than 20 years, while Neptune is the windeest planet on the solar system.

How were the planets formed?

After the gaseous cloud the "solar nebula" (a bright area in the night sky that is caused by a mass of dust or gas or by a large cloud of stars that are far away) collapsed upon itself due to the strength of its own gravity and formed the Sun, the dust particles around it clumped together to form the planets. The heat of the Sun melted the ice particles nearby and eventually these rocks grew larges to form the four rocky planets. Same ice particles were too far away from the Sun to be melted. These ice pieces combined with gases to form the planets called the gas giants.

Dwarf Planets

In 2006, the International Astronomical Union (IAU) made a decision that changed the way we organize our solar system. The IAU announced the removal of Pluto from the list of planets. They reclassified Pluto as a dwarf planet. Intead of nine, we now have only 8 planets in our solar system.
